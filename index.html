Prior Year Doc Upload & Extract

Reducing Abandonment Through AI-Powered Onboarding

Intuit · TurboTax Canada

🏆 Founder's Innovation Award Nominee

+4pts early funnel conversion · +5pts NPS · 90% of return pre-filled

Built TurboTax Canada's first AI-powered onboarding experience — using LLMs to extract and pre-fill 90% of a user's return from their prior year tax data, ensuring no credits or deductions are missed for maximum refund accuracy.

The Problem

Business Problem Intuit was spending acquisition budget to bring new users to TurboTax Canada — and losing 15% of them before they ever experienced the product. With growth targets anchored on converting users who previously filed with accountants, every dollar spent acquiring users who abandoned in onboarding was wasted.

Customer Problem The initial assumption was that confusing UI was driving drop-off. I conducted customer research to dig deeper — and it told a different story. New users weren't confused by the UI, they were anxious about starting from scratch. Returning users stayed because their prior year data was already there. New users left because it wasn't.

Strategy & Approach

  • Reframed the solution space from UI optimisation to data continuity — give new users the same experience returning users had
  • Let users upload last year's tax return; used LLMs to pre-fill ~90% of their profile data from it
  • Chose the richer T1 tax return over simpler alternate documents — validated through user research that data richness outweighed accessibility for the switcher segment
  • Partnered with marketing to launch a targeted switching campaign, using the feature as the centrepiece of acquisition efforts aimed at accountant-reliant filers

Technical Details

  • Two-stage LLM pipeline: first model classifies the document to confirm it's the correct document; second LLM extracts the fields and maps them to TurboTax's data model
  • First AI-powered experience in TurboTax Canada's history
  • Reduced latency by working with engineering to design a real-time synchronous extraction flow — contributed back as a reusable platform asset
  • Built an automated feedback loop to measure precision and recall of the extraction model post-launch, enabling continuous accuracy improvement without manual review overhead
  • Designed the data layer so imported fields dynamically suppress redundant downstream questions — foundation for a fully AI-driven onboarding experience

AI-Powered Expense Extraction for Self-Employed Filers

Intuit · TurboTax Canada

+20% conversion YoY · -15% time to complete · +$500 average refund amount

Self-employed filers spend hours manually entering and categorizing business expenses — often unsure which tax category applies, risking missed deductions and a smaller refund, and leading many to abandon DIY software altogether in favour of accountants. This feature extracts expenses directly from messy, unstructured spreadsheets and auto-categorizes them to the correct tax categories using LLMs — eliminating manual effort, reducing errors, and maximizing the user's refund.

The Problem

Business Problem Self-employed users represent TurboTax Canada's highest-revenue segment — but the complexity of claiming business expenses, where a wrong category can trigger a government audit or cost them deductions, meant most defaulted to accountants rather than risk getting it wrong in DIY software. Winning this segment was critical to long-term revenue growth.

Customer Problem Research confirmed the root cause: the expense section required significant manual data entry, and selecting the correct tax category was genuinely difficult — users didn't know which category applied, and the consequence of getting it wrong felt too high. That combination of effort and anxiety was driving abandonment and keeping accountant-reliant filers from switching to DIY altogether.

Strategy & Approach

  • Used research sessions as a deliberate pre-alignment tool — got the entire cross-functional org to witness the customer pain before the investment conversation began
  • Structured delivery in two independent phases: LLM-powered spreadsheet upload first to validate adoption and collect real customer expense samples — which directly informed model training for phase 2 — then LLM auto-categorization built on that foundation to eliminate manual expense mapping entirely
  • Launched each phase as an independent A/B experiment — results from phase 1 validated the business case before committing engineering resources to the more complex auto-categorization build
  • Designed the capability as a scalable platform asset — the extraction and categorization infrastructure was built to extend beyond personal tax to business tax filing
  • Positioned this as phase one of a multi-year strategy toward year-round expense tracking — laying the foundation for an always-on financial management experience for self-employed users

Technical Details

  • Built the golden dataset for auto-categorization using real customer expense samples collected in phase 1, supplemented with synthetic data to cover edge cases — used for pre-launch evals measuring precision, recall, and category accuracy, and to iteratively fine-tune the categorization prompt before a line of production code was written
  • Championed an open-source model over a proprietary internal alternative — evaluated across cost, accuracy, latency, and scalability
  • Set an 80% confidence threshold across all 22 CRA tax categories — predictions below threshold were surfaced as uncategorized rather than shown to users, protecting refund accuracy. Achieved 80%+ on every category pre-launch using the golden dataset, and accuracy held on real production data post-launch, validated through an automated feedback loop.

03 / 03

ML-Powered Upsell Personalisation & New Service Design

Intuit · TurboTax Canada

Showed 33% fewer upsell prompts — and still increased conversions by ~50% · improved NPS

Identified a market gap created by AI, designed a net-new expert service tier at a lower price point, and built an ML model to ensure the right offer reached the right user at the right moment in their filing journey.

The Problem

Business Problem Expert upsells were a major revenue stream for TurboTax Canada — but conversion was declining and the one-size-fits-all approach was generating customer complaints. Every user saw the same upsell triggers regardless of their tax complexity or confidence level.

Customer Problem Research, deep quantitative analysis across user tax profiles, and industry research revealed the real picture: high-confidence users were being interrupted by upsells they didn't need; users who genuinely needed help weren't being reached at the right moment; and the rise of AI chat tools had shifted behaviour — users were getting tax questions answered by AI, making unlimited expert access feel overpriced for what they now needed. The real insight: users still valued expert validation, they just didn't need unlimited access to it. The market had moved and the product hadn't.

Strategy & Approach

  • Identified a market gap created by AI and designed a net-new service: a single expert review call at a lower price point — expert confidence without unlimited access
  • The critical constraint was cannibalisation — users likely to buy the full service should never see the lower-priced option, or revenue would drop
  • Built an ML model specifically to solve this: score each user's likelihood to need full vs. light-touch expert help, and suppress the wrong offer accordingly
  • Optimised trigger timing — surfacing the right offer at the right moment, whether at review or when a balance-owing threshold was crossed

Technical Details

  • ML model scored each return across three dimensions: likelihood to stay self-serve, likelihood to need full expert assistance, likelihood to convert on the lighter-touch service
  • Trained on two years of prior tax return data with 50+ attributes — tax complexity, refund amount, filing experience, employment type, marital status, number of dependants
  • Showed 33% fewer upsell prompts to users — and still increased conversion by ~50% — proving that precision targeting outperforms volume
